1. Moms Mabley, (1894-1975)

Moms Mabley, born Loretta Aiken, was an African American comedian that broke through the barriers set in place to deter women especially black women from the comedy world. Her childhood was rough and growing up she faced many challenges and tribulations but that didnâ€™t stop her from pursuing her dream. She worked hard and went on to be featured on stage at Apollo Theater as the first woman with such an opportunity.

2. Charles Follis, (1879-1910)

Charles Follis was the first African American professional football player. He played football in high school and was a gifted athlete who joined an amateur league in college. Though his life was cut short due to an unfortunate illness his contributions to football and the significance of being the first African American player led the way for other African American men to participate in the sport.

3. Althea Gibson, (1927-2003)

Before the introduction of Venus and Serena Williams into the tennis world was Althea Gibson well-known for her incredible skills and powerful plays. She broke down the race walls of the segregated sport in the 1950s and established herself by winning multiple local and regional tournaments. In the end, all her hard work paid off as she became the first Black player to win a Grand Slam tournament, the French Championships. Though her story does not stop there. After retiring from tennis she began competing in professional golf and became the first Black player on the LPGA tour yet another reason for the importance she played in establishing a status for African American women in sports.
