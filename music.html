1. Henry Ossawa Tanner, (1859-1937)

Henry Ossawa Tanner was the first African American painter to get immense recognition during the 1800s. His artwork focused on scenes inspired by the stories in the bible which was influenced by his upbringing as a minister’s son. Still today, his paintings remain valuable in our society and are showcased in prestigious museums including the Smithsonian American Art Museum.

2. Gerald Wilson, (1918-2014)

Gerald Wilson was a major innovator in jazz music with his remarkable compositions and work as an orchestra director. As a child, he learned piano from his mother and began his journey in the music world as a trumpet player. He played among other well-known artists like Duke Ellington and Ella Fitzgerald and thrived in his career to one day be able to record his music on the prestigious Pacific Jazz label. Lastly, he has been recognized for his many accomplishments but having his arrangements achieved by the Library of Congress and was honored by the National Endowment of the Arts with a Jazz Masters Award.

3. Gordon Parks, (1912-2006)

Gordon Park’s list of achievements is extensive, to say the least, as he was a jazz pianist, who composed musical scores, wrote 15 books and co-founded Essence magazine. However, the list doesn't stop there as he is also highly regarded for his photography. He traveled the country to capture the harsh reality of the segregated South and the events that took place during the civil rights movement all of which remain placed in major art museums today.
