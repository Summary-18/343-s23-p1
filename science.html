1. Mae Jemison, (1956-Present)

Mae Jemison is famous for becoming the first Black woman in space in 1992, on the second launch of the Space Shuttle Endeavor. She is a well-renowned astronaut who started attending Stanford University at the young age of 16 and went on to earn her Doctorate of Medicine from Cornell University in 1981.

2. Frederick McKinley Jones, (1893-1961)

Frederick McKinley Jones was an insightful African American man who is most well-known for his patent for a refrigeration system for vehicles that soon turned into the company, Thermo King. His invention created many new opportunities in a variety of areas like hospitals which were now able to get shipments of blood and vaccines. During his lifetime, he earned more than 60 patents and even after his death became the first African American to receive the National Medal of Technology in 1991.

3. Marie Maynard Daly, (1921-2003)

Marie Maynard Daly was a female chemist who became the first African American woman to obtain a PhD in chemistry in the United States. Her work focused on studying sugars, cholestrol, and proteins specifically to how they effect the functionality of the heart. In addition, she had an extensive teaching career working at Howard University, Columbia University in their College of Physicians and Surgeons, and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
